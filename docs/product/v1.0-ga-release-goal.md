# Runory 1.0 GA Release Goal — 90% Product Coverage, 10% Governed Extension

Status: Proposed product contract  
Date: 2026-07-03  
Target release: `Runory Cloud 1.0 — General Availability`  
Demand baseline: [Commercial FSM Customer Demand Benchmark](../research/commercial-fsm-customer-demand-benchmark.md)  
Milestone roadmap: [Runory Product Roadmap](./product-roadmap.md)

## 1. Executive Release Contract

Runory 1.0 is ready for General Availability when this statement is true and evidenced:

> Runory's official product, modules, templates, supported connectors, and
> governed runtime can complete at least 90% of the weighted business jobs in
> one canonical Reactive Repair / Callout journey at a production-shaped
> standard. Runory or an independent third-party developer can complete the
> remaining customer/industry-specific 10% through supported configuration,
> MCP, Skills, SDK modules/packs, connectors, and data tools—without modifying,
> bypassing, or forking Runory core.

This is a product-coverage and extensibility promise, not a count of screens or database objects.

The 1.0 claim contains two obligations:

```text
90% product obligation
  Runory must already provide the stable business capabilities shared by target customers.

10% extension obligation
  customer-specific differences must fit documented, secure, upgrade-safe extension paths.
```

If a customer requires a core-code fork, direct database mutation, duplicated workflow/form runtime, or a private replacement of a fundamental Runory aggregate, the 10% extension obligation has failed.

## 2. Target Customer Boundary

The 90% claim applies to one commercial operating model:
Reactive Repair / Callout Service.

### 2.1 Canonical Commercial Shape

Examples:

```text
HVAC repair
plumbing
electrical callout
waterproof repair
equipment repair
```

Typical journey:

```text
customer request / Voice
-> customer identification and triage
-> Quote / estimate / authorization
-> Work Order
-> planning, dispatch and Visit
-> onsite diagnosis, Forms, work, evidence and service report
-> review/rework and completion
-> Invoice / payment / refund where required
-> aftercare and warranty follow-up
```

Industry vocabulary and configuration may vary, but every 1.0 reference
implementation must preserve this same commercial lifecycle and authoritative
aggregates.

### 2.2 Explicitly Excluded Commercial Shapes

The following are post-1.0 product discovery, not residual 10% customization:

```text
Recurring Route / Maintenance Service
  Service Plans, recurrence, generated route work, pause/skip/renewal,
  recurring billing and route-cycle operations

Project / Installation Service
  project stages, milestones, dependencies, commissioning,
  progress billing and long-running project controls
```

They may reuse Runory's Foundation, but 1.0 does not claim that extensions can
turn the canonical product into either operating model without new official
product work.

### 2.3 What The Claim Does Not Mean

Runory 1.0 does not claim 90% coverage for every enterprise, regulated industry, country, ERP replacement, or arbitrary low-code application.

These require separate evidence and product contracts:

```text
large-enterprise workforce management
deep manufacturing/service-parts ERP
regulated medical/public-safety field operations
country-specific accounting/tax compliance
complex construction project controls
full contact-center replacement
unrestricted custom application development
```

## 3. Meaning Of The 90%

### 3.1 Unit Of Measurement

The unit is a normalized business job, not a feature-list sentence.

Examples:

```text
valid job:
  dispatcher assigns and reschedules a qualified technician without losing history

not a valid unit:
  calendar page

valid job:
  technician captures required evidence and cannot complete work until validation passes

not a valid unit:
  photo field
```

Jobs are normalized from the customer-demand benchmark and grouped by actor, trigger, intended outcome, exceptions, and evidence.

### 3.2 Coverage Standard

Only capabilities at `L3 production-shaped` or above receive full coverage credit.

```text
L0 unsupported                          0.00
L1 modeled/CRUD                         0.20
L2 primary happy path                   0.60
L3 production-shaped                    1.00
L4 scaled/optimized                     1.00 plus quality evidence
```

L3 requires, where applicable:

```text
authoritative state and domain invariant
named command/API contract
permission and assignment enforcement
validation and required evidence
reverse/exception operations
idempotency and concurrency behavior
durable audit/history
failure visibility, retry, recovery and diagnostics
mobile/desktop/portal experience in promised scope
migration, compatibility and tenant-isolation tests
measured performance/load profile
```

An installable module, object schema, generated CRUD page, prototype, or internal demo is not L3.

### 3.3 Weighting

For each target customer profile:

```text
Must job       weight 3
Important job  weight 2
Optional job   weight 1
```

Weighted product coverage:

```text
sum(job weight × released capability coverage factor)
------------------------------------------------------
sum(job weight)
```

Regulatory, tenant-isolation, financial-correctness, or safety blockers remain explicit gates even when their numerical weight is small.

### 3.4 Product Coverage Versus Solution Coverage

The release report must publish both:

```text
Product coverage
  official Runory product/configuration/supported connectors only

Solution coverage
  product coverage plus approved customer/industry extensions
```

The 1.0 target is:

```text
product coverage  >= 90% for the canonical reactive-repair job matrix
solution coverage = 100% of agreed Must jobs for each reference implementation
core forks         = 0
direct DB bypass   = 0
```

## 4. The Official 90% Product Baseline

The following are product responsibilities. Their absence cannot normally be reclassified as customer customization.

### 4.1 SaaS Trust And Operations

```text
organization/workspace/user identity
strict tenant isolation across UI/API/MCP/agent/job paths
authentication and session lifecycle
business roles, permissions and assignment checks
append-only audit and domain history
API keys/service identity where supported
idempotency and optimistic concurrency
outbox/retry/dead-letter diagnostics
backup/restore, export, retention and deletion operations
rate limits, structured errors and observability
catalog/version/migration/rollout safety
```

### 4.2 CRM And Customer Context

```text
Lead, Contact, Company/Customer and Opportunity
multiple properties/service sites per customer
assets/equipment and installed-base context
ownership, assignment, qualification and conversion
notes, activities, files and governed relations
customer/site/asset operational service history
search, saved operational views and export
```

### 4.3 Commercial Operations

```text
product/service catalog and price-book items
typed Quote with composite lines and server calculations
Quote revision, approval, return, rejection, withdrawal and acceptance
immutable commercial snapshots
formal branded print/PDF output
idempotent Quote-to-Work-Order conversion
typed Contract and Invoice documents
single-job deposit/final invoice and payment schedule where required
payment-status ledger and reconciliation boundary
```

Runory does not need to become a general ledger, but it must provide a trustworthy commercial handoff and integration boundary.

### 4.4 FSM Execution

```text
Work Order, Visit/booking and service tasks
manual dispatch and assignment lifecycle
resource/technician/crew profile and availability
calendar, backlog, resource timeline and basic map
schedule conflicts, reassign and reschedule
mobile My Work and personal schedule
start/travel/arrive/execute/submit/complete actions
Forms, inspections, checklists, evidence, signature/acknowledgment
service report/deliverable review, return and resubmit
cancel, block, reopen and other governed exceptions
deadlines, overdue state and basic escalation
```

### 4.5 Repair History And Follow-through

```text
customer/site/asset repair history
warranty and aftercare context
recommended next action without automatic recurring generation
follow-up ownership, due date, status and audit
repair-relevant skills, territory, capacity and availability context
```

### 4.6 Mobile And Offline Field Work

```text
installable, fast mobile PWA
camera/file/signature interaction
safe weak-network and update recovery
explicit offline dataset scope
encrypted device store and session binding
offline form/evidence/command queue
resumable upload
conflict detection/resolution and sync diagnostics
```

A native wrapper/application is optional if the PWA satisfies the measured work. Reliable offline execution is not optional for customer profiles whose Must jobs require it.

### 4.7 Customer Operations And Communication

```text
customer portal identity and scoped access
document/service-history access
support Ticket and callout booking
surveys/review requests
notification templates, reminders and delivery history
provider-neutral outbound/two-way email/SMS boundary
telephony and human-handoff boundary
consent, preference and communication audit
```

### 4.8 Inventory And Procurement

```text
parts/material/chemical catalog and units
warehouse and truck/vehicle stock locations
reservation, issue, return, adjustment and count
job consumption
purchase request/order/receipt workflow
replenishment signal and history
```

Industry dosage or billing rules may remain in packs, but the inventory truth cannot be a customer-specific spreadsheet integration.

### 4.9 Integration Platform

```text
provider-neutral connector contracts
OAuth/API credential and secret governance
webhook verification and replay protection
idempotent import/export/synchronization
mapping and reconciliation state
health, retry, dead-letter and operator diagnostics
supported payment, accounting, messaging, signature and map-provider classes
```

Runory need not officially implement every provider. It must make provider adapters safe and repeatable.

### 4.10 Operational AI

```text
authorized knowledge/history retrieval
technician and support assistance
operational summaries and anomaly signals
route/dispatch recommendations
AI phone/email/communication adapter boundary
Workflow agents that call governed commands
human approval and escalation policy
prompt/model/tool version traceability
evaluation, feedback, cost and safety telemetry
```

AI cannot be counted as product coverage when it merely generates text without a grounded, permission-safe business outcome.

### 5.2 Industry Packs

```text
HVAC diagnostic measurements and equipment templates
landscaping zones and seasonal programs
pest-treatment policy and evidence
aquarium water-parameter workflows
```

### 5.3 Customer/Provider Connectors

```text
regional accounting provider
```

### 5.4 Data Work


## 6. What Must Not Be Hidden In The 10%

The following indicate product incompleteness when broadly required by the target profile:

```text
building a second workflow or forms engine
adding a new approval object for each domain
rewriting authentication/tenant isolation
directly changing governed status fields
custom dispatch/schedule truth outside Planning runtime
customer-specific offline synchronization engine
one-off audit or retry infrastructure
custom invoice/stock/payment truth because the shared aggregate is absent
forking a core module to add a normal reverse operation
duplicating desktop records in a mobile-only backend
unreviewed agent/database access that bypasses commands
```

If the same extension is requested by several customers, Runory must reassess whether it belongs in the official 90% baseline or a maintained industry pack.

## 7. Extension Capability Model

MCP and Skills are important parts of the 10% model, but they are not the entire extension platform.

### 7.1 Configuration And Metadata

Best for declarative customer differences:

```text
fields, relations, terminology
views and layouts
Forms and bindings
Workflow definitions and policies
roles/permission groups
document/report templates
```

Configuration must be schema-validated, versioned, previewable, auditable, and upgrade-compatible.

### 7.2 MCP

MCP exposes discoverable, permission-aware product capabilities to agents and external tools.

MCP tools MUST:

```text
resolve tenant/principal context
call the same query and command contracts as the product
enforce permissions and expected versions
support idempotency for mutation
return structured errors and audit identity
avoid raw table/SQL access
```

Representative uses:

```text
inspect customer/work history
create or update safe draft data
execute an authorized business command
import/validate/migrate data through staged jobs
diagnose failed integrations
configure Forms/Workflow through governed management commands
```

### 7.3 Skills

Skills package repeatable operating knowledge around product capabilities.

Representative uses:

```text
customer data migration playbook
vertical configuration procedure
quote/catalog setup
reactive-repair implementation and acceptance
connector installation and verification
incident/diagnostic workflow
industry-specific AI assistant behavior
```

A Skill may orchestrate MCP/API/CLI tools. It must not become a hidden substitute for missing server invariants or embed credentials/customer secrets.

### 7.4 Module And Pack SDK

The SDK supports typed business extensions that require persistent objects, commands, views, policies, migrations, or default configuration.

It MUST provide:

```text
typed manifests and contracts
dependency/compatibility declarations
namespaced persistence and migrations
extension-point validation
permission declaration and review
test harness and sample workspace
package/sign/publish/install/upgrade/uninstall lifecycle
data-retention and rollback policy
```

### 7.5 Connector SDK

Connector extensions require additional primitives:

```text
OAuth/credential lifecycle
secret storage and rotation
webhook verification
rate-limit/backoff policy
idempotency and replay
mapping/reconciliation state
outbox/dead-letter operations
health and operator diagnostics
PII/data-residency declarations
```

### 7.6 Data Tooling

Data extensions use staged jobs:

```text
extract -> map -> validate -> dry run -> approve -> import
-> reconcile -> report conflicts -> retry/rollback where safe
```

Every job is workspace-scoped, resumable, auditable, and exportable. Bulk maintenance uses the command catalog for governed changes.

## 8. Third-Party Developer Experience Contract

The 10% promise is credible only if a capable developer outside the core Runory team can deliver it.

Runory 1.0 MUST provide:

```text
public extension architecture and boundary documentation
versioned API/MCP/SDK contracts
local developer environment and sample data
extension scaffolding and validation
test harness for tenant isolation, permissions, migration and compatibility
sandbox workspace installation
upgrade preflight and compatibility report
observability/logs for owned extensions
publishing/signing/distribution path
security review requirements
support policy and deprecation windows
```

Documentation must include complete examples for at least:

1. one industry pack using Forms, Workflow and typed objects;
2. one external connector using OAuth/webhooks/outbox/reconciliation;
3. one MCP + Skill data migration/maintenance workflow;
4. one AI assistant constrained to approved commands and customer knowledge.

## 9. Compatibility And Upgrade Promise

Runory 1.0 cannot claim an extension ecosystem if normal upgrades break customer solutions unpredictably.

Required guarantees:

```text
semantic versions for public contracts
declared compatibility ranges
immutable published artifacts
upgrade preflight and migration dry run
running Workflow/Form version pinning
retained customer data on extension uninstall by policy
documented breaking-change/deprecation window
rollback or forward-fix plan
extension health after core upgrade
```

The release test must upgrade representative v0.9 solutions to 1.0 while preserving records, submissions, decisions, schedules, audit history, connector state, and custom extensions.

## 10. Validation Cohort

The GA coverage claim requires multiple independent implementations of the
same canonical commercial shape, not multiple commercial archetypes.

Minimum v1.0 evidence cohort:

```text
>= 15 independent customer/prospect profiles
all profiles mapped to the Reactive Repair / Callout job matrix
>= 3 compatible repair/service industries represented where practical
>= 3 production-like reference implementations/pilots
>= 2 extensions implemented by developers outside the core feature author
XLink operating case retained as a deep exception/acceptance reference
mature-vendor evidence retained for major domain boundaries
```

Suggested release thresholds:

```text
weighted product coverage across cohort       >= 90%
median individual profile product coverage    >= 90%
canonical journey-stage coverage              >= 90%
agreed Must jobs after supported extension    = 100%
security/tenant/regulatory release blockers   = 0
core forks used by reference solutions        = 0
```

These thresholds may be revised before v0.9 only through an explicit product decision recorded with evidence. They may not be weakened quietly to make the release pass.

## 12. GA Release Gates

### 12.1 Product Coverage

- validation cohort and normalized-job matrix are complete;
- coverage thresholds in Section 10 pass;
- all counted Must capabilities are L3 or higher;
- the canonical reactive-repair journey passes end to end for every maintained
  reference configuration;
- the customer-demand benchmark is current.

### 12.2 Extension Proof

- three distinct residual-10% cases are completed without core modification;
- at least two are implemented/reproduced by a third-party or non-core author from published documentation;
- one data migration, one connector, and one industry-policy extension pass;
- install, upgrade, disable, and uninstall/retention behavior is verified;
- MCP/Skill operations preserve permission, audit and command invariants.

### 12.3 Trust And Operations

- tenant-isolation matrix passes for UI/API/MCP/agent/job/connector paths;
- backup/restore and disaster-recovery drills pass;
- governed mutations are idempotent and concurrency-safe;
- outbox/dead-letter failures are visible and recoverable;
- offline synchronization cannot silently lose/duplicate business actions;
- connector secrets, webhooks and consent paths pass security review;
- AI evaluation, permission and human-handoff gates pass;
- performance and scale budgets pass with published load profiles.

### 12.4 Merchant Payments

- Runory SaaS subscription billing and Workspace merchant payments remain
  separate domains, credentials, webhooks, records, and settlement paths;
- each production Workspace merchant receives customer payments through its own
  provider account;
- the Stripe implementation uses Connect Direct Charges and does not settle
  merchant gross receipts into the Runory platform balance;
- onboarding, account restrictions, charge capability, refunds, disputes,
  balances, payouts, reconciliation, and support paths are production-shaped;
- cross-Workspace isolation passes for Connected Account onboarding, Checkout,
  webhook, refund, dispute, payout, and reconciliation operations;
- a production-shaped Sandbox E2E and an approved limited live-money drill are
  recorded before launch.

The detailed gate is defined in the
[Stripe Connect Pre-GA Completion Plan](stripe-connect-pre-ga-plan.md).

### 12.5 Upgrade And Support

- representative v0.9 solutions upgrade successfully;
- public contracts and deprecation/support policies are published;
- product, extension, connector and incident documentation is complete;
- supported browser/mobile/deployment matrix is declared;
- known limits and non-goals are explicit;
- operational ownership and escalation paths exist.

## 14. Anti-Goals

Runory 1.0 is not defined by:

```text
number of modules or Marketplace items
number of AI features
ability to generate arbitrary CRUD applications
one successful XLink migration
one bespoke customer implementation
feature parity with every Salesforce/Microsoft/ServiceNow product
support for every accounting, payment, mapping or communication provider
custom SQL access presented as extensibility
```

## 15. Ongoing Governance

Before 1.0, every formal roadmap review should ask:

```text
Which normalized customer jobs became stronger or weaker signals?
Which L1/L2 capabilities are being mistaken for commercial coverage?
Which repeatedly requested customizations should move into official product or a maintained pack?
Which extensions are pressuring teams to fork core?
Can an external developer reproduce the documented 10% path?
Are official capabilities still coherent across desktop, mobile, portal, API, MCP and agent?
Does an upgrade preserve customer data, process history and extension behavior?
```

After 1.0, the same benchmark and gates continue. GA is the beginning of a compatibility and customer-outcome promise, not the end of product learning.

## 16. Final Product Judgment

The 90/10 model is feasible because Runory is deliberately building reusable execution primitives rather than one fixed FSM application.

It remains feasible only under three disciplines:

1. shared customer needs become high-quality official product capabilities;
2. customer/industry variation uses governed extension points rather than core forks;
3. coverage is continuously measured against real customer work at L3, not asserted from feature names.

The defining 1.0 test is therefore:

> Can Runory and an independent developer deliver the target customer's complete agreed operation—90% from released Runory capabilities and 10% from supported extensions—while preserving security, business invariants, auditability, upgrades, and operational support?

When the answer is repeatedly yes for the canonical Reactive Repair / Callout
shape across independent customers and compatible industries, Runory is ready
for 1.0 General Availability.
