## Release outcome

v0.7 completes the commercial ending of the existing Reactive Repair / Callout
journey:

```text
accepted Quote
â†’ governed Work Order and field execution
â†’ completed Work Order
â†’ issued Invoice
â†’ payment request and provider-confirmed allocation
â†’ paid / outstanding / refunded visibility
```

The release adds the smallest missing financial document and connects it to the
Payment capability already shipped in v0.5. It does not reopen the v0.6
Foundation or introduce a second commercial operating model.

## Evidence and gap decision

The repository audit found that Runory already has governed Quote approval and
acceptance, Quote-to-Work-Order conversion, dispatch and Visit execution,
Forms/evidence, service reports, Work Order completion, Stripe-hosted payment,
refunds, and aftercare follow-up.

The material missing link is a durable customer receivable between completed
work and payment. Direct payment against a Quote or Work Order is useful, but it
does not provide an issued document, a stable amount due, or payment allocation
visibility.

## Explicit non-goals

- Contract or e-signature;
- general ledger, accounting export, tax calculation, credit notes, or
  jurisdiction-specific invoice compliance;
- recurring, progress, consolidated, or project billing;
- arbitrary Invoice creation or free-form line editing;
- split tender, partial payment plans, or manual bank reconciliation;
- a public customer portal or general-purpose document-sharing platform;
- inventory/material management;
- Agent-generated official Module/Pack/Template productization;
- new Foundation abstractions without a demonstrated v0.7 requirement.

## Product rules

1. A Work Order must be `completed` before its Invoice can be issued.
2. A Work Order can have at most one active Invoice; retry returns the same
   Invoice.
3. A Quote-origin Work Order uses the accepted Quote's immutable commercial
   snapshot.
4. An Invoice cannot be voided after any successful payment has been allocated.
5. A payment or refund changes Invoice settlement only after a trusted provider
   event succeeds.
6. All monetary persistence uses integer minor units and one currency per
   Invoice.
7. Payment allocation is bounded by the Invoice balance; overpayment is
   rejected.

## Definition of done

v0.7 is complete only when:

- the canonical journey reaches a `paid` Invoice through the real Payment
  command/provider-event path;
- a full refund reopens the Invoice receivable correctly;
- duplicate issue, payment event, and refund event delivery are idempotent;
- generic CRUD cannot create or mutate governed Invoice financial state;
- operators can find outstanding Invoices and act from the normal workspace;
- the Architecture Inventory reports no missing or duplicate Command Contract
  or Provider;
- the full repository quality gates pass;
- no admitted item requires Contract, recurring service, accounting, customer
  portal, or public MPT-generation abstractions.

## Deferred after v0.7

Customer document access remains a v1.0 journey requirement, but it should be
designed from observed customer communication needs rather than hidden inside
Invoice delivery. Agent discovery and official MPT generation remain the v0.8
productization theme. Contract, advanced receivables, inventory, and additional
commercial shapes remain uncommitted later candidates.
